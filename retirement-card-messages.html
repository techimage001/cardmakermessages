Retirement Card Messages for Colleagues and Friends

Retirement card messages should recognise contribution, character and the new freedom ahead. Thank the person for something specific, mention the difference they made and wish them a fulfilling next chapter. Humour works best when it celebrates freedom without suggesting the person is old or no longer useful.

What do I write in a retirement card?

Begin with congratulations, then mention the person’s service, example or support. Close with a wish for health, rest, family time, travel or meaningful new activities. A group card can be brief because many people will add messages, while a close colleague can write more personally.

  1. Congratulate the person on retiring.
  2. Thank them for a specific contribution.
  3. Mention a quality or memory colleagues will value.
  4. Wish them enjoyment and purpose in the next chapter.

What should I write to a retiring colleague?

Thank your colleague for a real contribution, such as their reliability, humour, mentoring or calm approach. Mention that they will be missed without making them feel guilty for leaving. End with a positive wish for how they might use their time. If the card is from a whole team, write clearly enough that the message still makes sense alongside many shorter notes.

What is a funny retirement message that is not rude?

Use humour about fewer alarms, no meetings or having time to choose what not to do. Avoid jokes about age, health, memory or being replaceable. The safest humour celebrates the person gaining freedom. Pair the joke with a sincere sentence of appreciation so the card still recognises their work and the relationship built with colleagues.

How do I write a retirement message for a boss?

Keep the wording respectful and specific. Thank the boss for guidance, trust, opportunities or the culture they helped create. Avoid exaggerated praise that may feel insincere. A useful message can say that their leadership made a lasting difference, that the team appreciates their support and that you wish them a happy and rewarding retirement.
