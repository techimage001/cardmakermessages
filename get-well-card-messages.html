Get Well Card Messages That Offer Real Support

Get well card messages should offer care without pressuring the recipient to recover quickly or reply. Say that you are thinking of them, wish them comfort and offer practical support when appropriate. For serious illness, avoid promises, medical advice and phrases that make the person responsible for staying positive.

How do I write a supportive get well message?

Use gentle wording that recognises the person rather than focusing only on the illness. You can say there is no need to respond and offer one specific form of help. Religious messages can include prayers for peace, skilled care and strength without promising a particular outcome.

  1. Tell the person you are thinking of them.
  2. Wish them comfort, rest or steady recovery.
  3. Offer practical help only when you can provide it.
  4. Remove any pressure to reply or recover on a timetable.

What else should I know?

What should I avoid writing in a get well card?

Avoid saying everything happens for a reason, promising a full recovery or comparing the person’s illness with someone else’s experience. Do not give treatment advice unless you are their qualified clinician and it is the proper setting. Avoid placing emotional work on the recipient by asking for updates in the card. Keep the message caring, realistic and focused on support.

What can I write to a colleague who is unwell?

Keep the message warm and professional. Say that the team is thinking of them and that work can wait while they focus on rest and recovery. Do not ask for medical details or promise when they will return. A group card can include a brief update about being missed, but avoid language that could make the person feel pressured to come back early.

Is it appropriate to add humour?

Humour can be appropriate for a minor illness when you know the person’s style well. Keep the joke gentle and never make fun of symptoms, treatment or appearance. For serious, uncertain or long-term illness, use warmth rather than humour unless the recipient has clearly welcomed it. A caring message is more important than trying to make the card entertaining.
