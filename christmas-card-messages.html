Christmas Card Messages for Family and Friends

Christmas card messages can be warm, religious, professional or playful depending on the recipient. A useful structure is a seasonal greeting, one sincere wish and a closing that fits your relationship. Business cards should thank the recipient without making assumptions about faith, while close family cards can be more personal.

How do I choose the right Christmas card wording?

Choose wording that fits both the relationship and the recipient’s traditions. Use a religious message when you know it will be welcomed. For customers, neighbours and wider groups, a peaceful seasonal message is usually safer. Personal cards become warmer when you mention distance, shared memories or gratitude for the year.

  1. Decide whether the message should be religious or general.
  2. Choose family, friendship or professional wording.
  3. Add thanks, a shared memory or a hope for the New Year.
  4. Sign with the names of everyone sending the card.

What else should I know?

What should a business Christmas card say?

A business Christmas card should thank the customer, employee or supplier and offer a positive seasonal wish. Keep the wording professional, inclusive and brief. Avoid sales language inside the greeting because it weakens the sense of appreciation. A suitable message might thank the recipient for their support and wish them a restful holiday season and continued success. Add the company or team name clearly at the end.

Should I write Merry Christmas or Season’s Greetings?

Use Merry Christmas when you know the recipient celebrates Christmas and is comfortable with the wording. Use Season’s Greetings or warm holiday wishes for mixed audiences, workplace groups and people whose traditions you do not know. Neither option is automatically better. The thoughtful choice is the one that respects the recipient while still sounding natural from the sender.
