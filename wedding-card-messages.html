Wedding Card Messages for the Happy Couple

Wedding card messages should celebrate the couple without making promises on their behalf. Offer congratulations, name a quality you admire in their relationship and wish them well for the life they are building. Religious wording can include a blessing, while formal cards should remain warm, respectful and concise.

What do I write in a wedding card?

Begin with congratulations, then add a wish for the marriage rather than only the wedding day. Close friends can mention a shared memory or how happy the couple seem together. For a colleague or distant relative, a polished two-sentence message is completely appropriate.

  1. Congratulate both partners.
  2. Wish them love, friendship and strength for married life.
  3. Add a personal detail only when it is suitable.
  4. Sign clearly so the couple knows who sent the card.

What else should I know?

Is it acceptable to write a short wedding message?

Yes. A short wedding message can be elegant and meaningful, especially when you are not closely connected to the couple. Two sentences are enough: offer congratulations and wish them happiness in their marriage. Add your name clearly. Longer messages are better reserved for close friends and family when you have something genuinely personal to say rather than filling space with general phrases.

What can I write in a religious wedding card?

A religious wedding card can ask for guidance, grace, unity and blessing in the couple’s marriage. Choose wording that fits their faith and avoid quoting a verse unless you are confident it is accurate and appropriate. An original blessing often feels more personal. For example, you can ask that their home be filled with patience, peace and love through every season.

Should the card be addressed to both people?

Address the card to both partners unless the card is intentionally personal from a parent or close relative to one person. Use the names and order the couple prefers. Inside the message, speak to them as a couple and avoid focusing only on the person you know. This helps the card feel welcoming and celebrates the marriage rather than one individual.
